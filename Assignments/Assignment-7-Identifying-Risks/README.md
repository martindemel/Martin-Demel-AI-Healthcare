# Assignment 7: Identifying Risks in a NICU Case Study

## Overview

This assignment analyzes a peer-reviewed case report of a female infant born at 24 weeks and 4 days' gestation (800 g) who developed severe necrotizing enterocolitis (NEC) with Clostridium neonatale bacteremia on day 21 of life.

## Case Summary

The infant initially required surfactant and nasal CPAP for respiratory distress syndrome. On day 21, she developed feeding intolerance, bilious stomach residuals, abdominal swelling, and rectal bleeding. Her X-ray showed pneumatosis intestinalis (air in the bowel wall), confirming severe NEC.

## Three Significant Risks Identified

### 1. Sepsis and Shock
The infant already had bacteria in the blood, and NEC can allow infection to spread rapidly. In an extremely preterm baby, delayed treatment could lead to organ failure or death. AI might assist by analyzing monitor data, lab results, and electronic records to alert staff earlier.

### 2. Bowel Perforation and Surgical Complications
Surgery revealed severe ischemic jejunal injury, and the baby required an ileostomy. If bowel damage is not recognized early, the intestine can perforate, leading to peritonitis and lasting digestive issues. AI-assisted image review could help identify worsening patterns sooner.

### 3. Poor Growth and Long-Term Developmental Issues
NEC is associated with later neurodevelopmental problems, and this infant required extended parenteral nutrition after surgery. AI could assist by monitoring growth, lab trends, and feeding tolerance over time.

## Key Takeaway

This case shows why risk identification is critical in healthcare. In the NICU, small warning signs can quickly become life-threatening. AI is most useful when it supports the care team, because early warning and improved planning can make care safer for fragile newborns.
