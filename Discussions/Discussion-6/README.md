# Discussion 6: Population Health Analytics and Surveillance

## Week Topic

Population health analytics, syndromic surveillance, and social determinants of health.

## Key Learnings

Population health analytics uses AI to look beyond one patient and spot patterns across communities in near real time. The CDC's BioSense Platform uses syndromic surveillance — tracking symptom patterns before diagnoses are confirmed — to detect outbreaks and support response. For NICU families, this matters because respiratory waves like influenza or RSV can raise admissions and readmissions, especially for premature infants. Social determinants of health, the conditions where people live and access care, can be modeled to explain disparities in outcomes like preterm birth and NICU admission. Malin and colleagues (2025) found social factors linked to poorer infant outcomes, showing why equity analytics must be part of population modeling. Bias can enter healthcare AI through data gaps and design choices, so transparency and subgroup checks are not optional.

## Reflection

When population health teams use real-time surveillance plus social determinants of health data to flag high-risk areas, how do they protect privacy in small communities while still giving NICUs actionable guidance, such as surge planning or targeted prevention for high-risk premature infants?
