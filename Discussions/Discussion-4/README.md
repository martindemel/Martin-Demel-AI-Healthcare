# Discussion 4: Computer Vision and Retinopathy of Prematurity

## Week Topic

Computer vision and deep learning for diagnostic AI in medical imaging.

## Key Learnings

Computer vision and deep learning are powering many diagnostic AI tools by learning patterns directly from medical images. Convolutional neural networks (CNNs) are useful because they learn edges, textures, and shapes without requiring hand-crafted features. In neonatal care, the image-first approach is being explored for screening conditions like retinopathy of prematurity (ROP). Ortiz and colleagues (2025) evaluated machine learning to identify ROP from smartphone-collected retinal videos, showing how computer vision could expand access when specialists are limited. Performance metrics are critical to interpret: sensitivity is the true positive rate, specificity is the true negative rate, and positive and negative predictive values shift with prevalence, which is especially important in smaller NICU populations. The area under the ROC curve (AUC) summarizes discrimination across thresholds, but the chosen threshold determines the real-world trade-off.

## Reflection

If a NICU adopts an imaging AI tool for screening, how should the team choose the operating threshold to maintain sensitivity while avoiding unnecessary follow-up exams and alert fatigue, and how often should that threshold be rechecked as prevalence and imaging quality change?
