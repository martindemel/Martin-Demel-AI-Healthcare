# Discussion 3: Clinical Decision Support in the NICU

## Week Topic

Clinical decision support systems (CDSS) and their evolution from rule-based alerts to AI-enabled predictive tools.

## Key Learnings

CDSS are tools embedded in the clinical workflow that use patient data and evidence to support decisions at the point of care. In the NICU, where one baby generates streams from bedside monitors, frequent labs, medication changes, ventilator adjustments, and imaging updates, CDSS can act as a second set of eyes and reduce errors. Traditional CDSS is often rule-based (weight-based dosing checks, drug interaction alerts, guideline reminders), but AI and machine learning are pushing CDSS into a more predictive space. Meeus and colleagues provided a concrete example: using continuous monitoring data to generate hourly risk predictions for late-onset sepsis and necrotizing enterocolitis with a low alarm rate. AI CDSS should be explainable and integrated into care pathways so it supports, not replaces, bedside judgment.

## Challenges

Distinguishing between traditional rule-based CDSS and AI-enabled predictive CDSS required understanding not just the technology but the clinical workflow implications — especially how alert fatigue can undermine even a well-built system.

## Reflection

In a real NICU workflow, how should teams set and adjust AI alert thresholds over time so early warning improves outcomes without increasing alarm fatigue, and what level of explanation should be required before clinicians act on an AI CDSS recommendation?
